Candidate Statements

Check back on January 24th for the beginning of the official campaign period, candidate statements will be uploaded then.

Running for Office

Any member in good standing may be elected to office. Members must be members of the California Young Democrats, namely persons from 13 to 35 who are registered Democrats or who intend to register when eligible. Join or update your membership registration. You may check your membership registration by inquiring with the Membership Director.

Officers serve from the close of the Annual Membership Meeting at which they are elected, until the close of the next Annual Membership Meeting. There are no term limits on holding office.

Office Descriptions

Chair

  1. The Chair shall serve as the chief executive officer and official voice of this organization.
  2. The Chair shall be the default presiding officer of this organization.
  3. The Chair shall serve as the representative of this organization at functions of all organizations with which this organization is affiliated unless a separate representative is otherwise appointed by the Chair.
  4. The Chair shall provide an overarching vision for this organization.
  5. The Chair shall have the authority to call meetings of this organization.
  6. The Chair shall be responsible for developing and fostering relationships between this organization and relevant members of the California Democratic Party and California Young Democrats.
  7. The Chair shall also serve as an ex-officio non-voting member on any committees for which the Chair is not a member.
  8. The Chair shall break ties of the Board or the Membership.

Political Director

  1. The Political Director shall ensure that the Caucus develops and implements a plan for political action - including lobbying, endorsements, and electioneering - reflecting the values of this organization.
  2. The Political Director shall work with Regional Directors to engage local CYD chapters and other interested organizations in political action.
  3. The Political Director shall serve as the chair for the Policy and Research Committee.

>Membership Director

  1. The Membership Director shall be responsible recruiting new members to the organization, and shall ensure the secretary promptly receives all such documents.
  2. The Membership Director shall work with the Regional Directors to hold regional membership meetings and recruit new members.
  3. The Membership Director shall assist the Chair in running Membership Meetings.
  4. The Membership Director shall be responsible for maintaining an accurate up up-to-date record of the membership of this organization.

Fundraising Director

  1. The Fundraising Director shall oversee all fundraising of this organization and communications with its donors.
  2. The Fundraising Director shall create and regularly and promptly update a database of this organization and its political donors.
  3. The Fundraising Director shall assist the Secretary-Controller and professional treasurer with all campaign finance disclosure documents.
  4. The Fundraising Director shall serve as the chair of the Finance Committee.

Communications Director

  1. The Communications Director shall assist the Chair in running this organization’s social media accounts.
  2. The Communications Director shall draft press releases for this organization in a timely manner at the request of the Chair or the Board.
  3. The Communications Director shall oversee the production of this organization’s no-less-than-quarterly newsletter.
  4. The Communications Director shall serve as this organization's default webmaster. The Communications Director may select a professional webmaster with the consent of the Board.

Secretary-Controller

  1. The Secretary-Controller shall serve as the default treasurer-of-record for this organization and its political committees, including filling out financial disclosure forms.  The Secretary-Controller may select a professional treasurer with the consent of the Board.
  2. The Secretary-Controller shall maintain all records and financial documents for this organization.
  3. The Secretary-Controller shall work with the Chair and the Fundraising Director to create maintain an annual budget for this organization, as well as to update it as necessary throughout the year.
  4. The Secretary-Controller shall give monthly updates to the Board on the finances of the organization and its political committees.
  5. The Secretary-Controller shall serve as the vice chair of the Finance Committee.
  6. The Secretary-Controller shall serve all required notices and perform any other duties directed by the Chair.

Regional Directors

  1. The Regional Director shall proactively follow political developments within throughout their region and make sure the Board and the membership are informed about those developments.
  2. The Regional Directors shall work with the Membership Director to hold quarterly regional membership meetings and recruit new members.
  3. The Regional Directors shall work with the Political Director to engage local CYD chapters and other interested organizations in political action.
  4. The Regional Directors shall seek-out and appoint, with the consent of the Board, Assistant Regional Directors to assist them with their duties.
  5. The Regional Directors shall be elected by the members of this organization who reside with their respective Regions. The Regions shall be as follows:
    1. The Northern Region consists of San Mateo, Santa Clara, Alameda, Contra Costa, Sacramento, Calaveras, and Alpine counties and all counties north of these counties.
    2. The Central Region consists of Santa Cruz, San Benito, Merced, Stanislaus, San Joaquin, Tuolumne, and Mono counties on the north boundary and Santa Barbara, Kern, Tulare, Fresno, and Mono counties on the south boundary and all counties in between.
    3. The Southern Region shall consist of Ventura, Los Angeles, San Bernardino and Inyo counties and all counties south of these counties.

Informational Videoconference

If you’re interested in seeking office, we will host a group video conference on Thursday, January 18th from 6-8 PM. You may join and leave whenever you like.

The purpose of this call is to provide an opportunity for you to ask questions of current board members, clarify any unclear or ambiguous portions of this elections document, meet other potential candidates, and gain the confidence to run.

The call will be hosted on Google Hangouts Meet at meet.google.com/vis-cdsw-dsm. As the number of simultaneous participants is limited to 25, please RSVP so we can anticipate turnout and host a second session if necessary.

Candidate Statements

When you declare your intent to run for office, you may submit a candidate bio, tagline, statement and headshot for inclusion on this page and in our other elections resources.

Three newsletters will be sent to the caucus membership in the campaign period as described below. As we have nine offices up for election and anticipate some competitive races, including the entirety of every candidate’s statement in each email newsletter is impractical. The newsletters will contain only a breakdown of candidates by race, each candidate’s name, and their tagline. The candidate’s name will be linked to their statement on this page.

Taglines may not exceed fifteen words.

The bio/statement should be no longer than 200 words.

You may elect to include a headshot with your statement, they will appear together on this page. For stylistic consistency, this should be a color portrait photo without any text or other manipulation superimposed.

No materials may contain links or URLs.

Revisions may be sent to Ben Isserlis at secretary.controller@cydprogressives.org. Online materials will only be updated as frequently as new declarations, that is, once per week on Wednesdays.

Questions from Members

In lieu of a debate (which would be costly, time consuming, interminably long, difficult to plan, and near impossible to coordinate between all candidates for all offices), we will begin accepting questions from members when the first campaign newsletter is sent out on January 24th.

Members will have until February 7th to submit questions for any particular office or for all candidates. We will then sort the questions and distribute the set to all candidates. Candidates will have until February 13th to submit their responses.

Responses will be accepted in the form of a PDF and linked from the candidate’s statement and in the second newsletter. Candidates may choose one question & answer pair to “feature”, which will be included directly in this page adjacent to their statement. The featured answer should be no longer than 200 words, or an excerpt may be designated. The featured answer may not include any links or URLs.

Though there is no restriction on the length of the provided PDF, candidates are advised to keep it succinct. We’re not here to read your novel and if you submit any fragment of War and Peace in jest we’ll make you wear a silly hat during the election.

Revisions will not be accepted once answers are posted.

Newsletters

We will distribute three newsletters during the campaign period from January 24th to February 21st.

Each newsletter will contain a list of all elections, all eligible candidates for each election, those candidates’ taglines, and a link to the candidate’s statement on this page.

The first newsletter will be sent on January 24th. In addition to the list of candidates, it will include a request for members to submit questions for candidates as described above.

The second newsletter will be sent on February 14th. In addition to the list of candidates, it will include the candidates’ responses to the member questions collected previously.

The third newsletter will be sent on February 21st and will mark the close of the campaign period at midnight. No further names will be collected for inclusion on the ballot or on this page, though members are of course allowed to be nominated from the floor and written in.

General Elections Information

Any registered member of the caucus present at the annual meeting at the California Young Democrats Convention in San Diego February 23-25 is eligible to vote in the caucus elections.

Register for Membership

If you have never registered or updated your registration online with us, please do so now! Our online registration form is much more comprehensive than any paper form or spreadsheet you may have filled out, and is the primary way to get involved with one of our caucuses as well.

Registration and attendance will be checked at the door. Only registered members may cast a ballot.

During the meeting, nominations will be opened and all verified candidates will be nominated en masse. Further nominations from the floor will be accepted.

Once nominations are closed, the Secretary will read the list of candidates in the order in which they must deliver their speech. Speeches will be ordered first by office, then alphabetically by last name. Candidates should form a line at the front of the room in the order in which they are to speak so that the process may move along promptly. As we anticipate having many candidates for our many offices, candidates should not expect to receive more than one minute for their speech.

Out of respect for all candidates, ballots will be distributed fifteen minutes from the end of the meeting or at the end of speeches, whichever comes first.

All expediency will be made in tallying the vote, however members should not be surprised to find the final determination announced by email some hours after the meeting has adjourned.

Volunteering

Seeing that the meeting runs smoothly and completes within a reasonable time takes coordinated effort on the part of many people. Thank you for your interest, and thank you for your help.

Volunteers are needed to

  • Register new members
  • Verify registrations and check all attendees in
  • Usher people to seats and maintain decorum
  • Distribute ballots
  • Count ballots
Volunteer at Convention

Volunteers must be available for 2 hours in the week before convention for training, and for 2-3 hours during convention for the meeting (setup, the program, cleanup).

More information will be posted as materials are finalized and dates set. Volunteers will be contacted with dates for videoconference training sessions in early February.
